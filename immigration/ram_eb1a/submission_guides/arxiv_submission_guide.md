## Why arXiv First

- arXiv preprints are accepted as Criterion 6 evidence by USCIS without requiring journal peer review acceptance
- arXiv goes live in 1–2 business days — build your Criterion 6 record NOW, not in 6 months
- Simultaneous journal submission is allowed after arXiv posting (submit to JAMIA immediately after)
- arXiv papers are indexed by Google Scholar — citations begin accumulating immediately
- arXiv is the standard first-publication venue in computer science and biomedical informatics

## Step-by-Step Submission

### Step 1 — Create an arXiv Account
1. Go to https://arxiv.org/user/register
2. Register with your institutional email (use ram.vadlamudi@ardiahealthlabs.com)
3. You will need an endorser for your first submission in cs.AI — see Step 6

### Step 2 — Prepare Your PDF
- Convert the `paper1_dgp_architecture.md` file to a formatted PDF
- Recommended: Use LaTeX (Overleaf.com — free) with the standard article template
- Alternative: Microsoft Word → PDF, ensuring professional formatting

- Required sections: Abstract, Introduction, Methods/Architecture, Results/Discussion, Conclusion, References

### Step 4 — Upload Files

Option A (LaTeX):
- Upload .tex source file
- Upload any figure files (.png, .pdf)
- arXiv compiles LaTeX automatically

Option B (PDF):
- Upload single PDF file
- arXiv accepts direct PDF submission

### Step 5 — Endorsement

For first-time submitters to cs.AI, arXiv requires an endorser — someone who has previously published in cs.AI on arXiv.

**How to get endorsed:**
1. Email a computer science faculty member at any university who has arXiv cs.AI papers
2. Subject: "arXiv cs.AI Endorsement Request — Healthcare Revenue Cycle AI Paper"
3. Include: your paper title, abstract, and arXiv account username
4. Most faculty respond within 48 hours

**Fastest path:** Contact UT Southwestern Medical Center informatics faculty or any DFW-area CS faculty. Alternatively, a former colleague at one of your prior employers (CIGNA, Teladoc Health, UHC) with arXiv papers can endorse.

### Step 6 — Submit

After endorsement is received:
1. Return to arXiv.org → My Submissions
2. Complete the submission (endorsement unlocks the Submit button)
3. You will receive a confirmation email with your arXiv ID (e.g., arXiv:2026.XXXXX)

### Step 7 — Save Your arXiv ID

Your arXiv ID is your EB-1A Criterion 6 evidence identifier.

Example: arXiv:2026.12345 [cs.AI]

Save this ID and include it in:
- Your EB-1A petition evidence package
- All subsequent papers (cite Paper 1 in Papers 2, 3, and 4)
- Your USCIS filing (print the arXiv listing page as evidence)

## After Publication — Immigration Use

**What to include in EB-1A petition:**
1. Print the arXiv listing page (shows your name, title, abstract, submission date, paper ID)
2. Print the full paper PDF
3. Include Google Scholar citation count once citations accumulate
4. Print any comments, downloads, or endorsements received

**Timeline note:** For immigration filing purposes, the arXiv submission date is the "publication" date. USCIS counts preprints as scholarly publications under Criterion 6.
