## Why This Won't Trigger RFEs

**On publication cadence:**
- One paper per month ALTERNATING looks like two researchers at a company who each have their own research portfolio. It does NOT look like a coordinated visa operation.
- LinkedIn articles in non-paper months look like authentic thought leadership, not manufactured evidence.

**On originality:**
- All 6 confirmed white spaces have been systematically searched (see research_gaps.md). No examiner can find prior art because there is none.
- DGP architecture is Ram's original invention. "Deterministic-Generative-Predictive" is not a term in prior literature.

**On expert witnesses:**
- The relationship-building approach (cite their work → share your preprint → have a conversation → ask for letter) looks authentic because it IS authentic. Examiners can tell when letters were written by people who actually know the petitioner's work.

**On the national importance argument:**
- $10–12B quantified problem + 35.3% denial rate (XiFin 2024, 20M+ claims) + 2.76× disparity (JAMA 2025) + 7,000 labs at risk + PAMA 2027 urgency = a documented national healthcare infrastructure crisis, not a speculative claim.

**On the "not merely a green card" appearance:**
- The platform (Ardia Health Labs) is a functioning company with pilot customers.
- The papers address real products (ToxIQ™, MolecuIQ™, AcoIQ™, BehaviorIQ™).
- The publication topics are exactly what a co-founder would document as they build these products.
- A USCIS examiner reading Paper 8 on Toxicology Billing AI will see it as "this person built ToxIQ™ and is documenting it" — not "this person is filling a visa checklist."
