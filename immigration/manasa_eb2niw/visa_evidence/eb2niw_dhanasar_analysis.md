## Overview

The Dhanasar framework establishes three prongs for National Interest Waiver eligibility. All three prongs are satisfied for Manasa Jampani:

1. **Prong 1 — Substantial Merit and National Importance**: The petitioner's proposed endeavor has substantial merit and national importance ✅ STRONG
2. **Prong 2 — Well Positioned to Advance the Endeavor**: The petitioner is well positioned to advance the proposed endeavor ✅ STRONG
3. **Prong 3 — On Balance, It Would Be Beneficial to the United States to Waive the Labor Certification Requirement**: It would be beneficial to the US to waive labor certification ✅ STRONG

## Prong 1: Substantial Merit and National Importance

### The Endeavor

Manasa Jampani's proposed endeavor is the co-development and commercialization of AI-native revenue cycle management infrastructure for independent clinical laboratories in the United States, specifically addressing claim denial recovery in the molecular diagnostics, pharmacogenomics, and next-generation sequencing segments.

### Substantial Merit

**Economic Merit**

The independent clinical laboratory billing domain represents an estimated $10–12 billion in annual preventable revenue loss attributable to claim denials that either go unappealed or fail due to inadequate documentation. This figure derives from two sources:

- XiFin 2024 Payor Denial Impact Report (20+ million claims): Molecular diagnostics denial rate of 35.3% — the highest across all healthcare specialties
- HFMA/LigoLab 2024 Laboratory Revenue Recovery Report: 65% of denied claims never appealed, despite 50–80.7% appeal win rates

The economic value of reducing this denial rate and closing the appeal gap is directly quantifiable: recovering even 10% of the estimated $10–12 billion annual loss would represent $1–1.2 billion in additional revenue annually to independent laboratories — laboratories that employ thousands of healthcare workers and provide essential diagnostic infrastructure.

**Clinical Merit**

Independent clinical laboratories are the diagnostic infrastructure for:
- Rural and underserved communities served by Critical Access Hospitals and independent clinics
- Addiction medicine practices relying on clinical toxicology panels for substance use disorder treatment monitoring (a national public health priority)
- Specialty oncology practices relying on NGS panels for precision cancer treatment selection
- Pharmacogenomics-guided prescribing, which offers the potential to reduce adverse drug events from medications with FDA-recognized pharmacogenomic biomarkers

When independent laboratories lose revenue to preventable claim denials and close or consolidate, these communities lose access to essential diagnostic services that are not replaced by hospital-based laboratories serving primarily inpatient and affiliated outpatient populations.

**Technical Merit**

The AI architecture being developed — the DGP (Deterministic-Generative-Predictive) Clinical Revenue Architecture, invented by Ram Vadlamudi — addresses a genuine technical problem that existing platforms cannot solve. The MolDX program complexity, DEX Z-code compliance requirements, and CPT 81400–81479 coding requirements for genomic sequencing represent a technical domain with no existing commercial AI solution. Manasa Jampani's contribution is the payer-side operational intelligence and business strategy that shapes the architecture's countermeasure design.

### National Importance

**Scale of Impact**

- $10–12 billion annual preventable revenue loss — a quantifiable economic burden on the US healthcare system
- 7,000+ CLIA-certified independent laboratories at financial risk
- More than 50% of independent laboratory directors considering consolidation or sale if PAMA 2027 reimbursement cuts take effect (ACLA 2024)
- PAMA 2027: Up to 45% cumulative Medicare reimbursement cut for clinical laboratory tests by 2029

**Healthcare Access Equity**

The 2.76× higher denial odds for independent vs. hospital-based laboratories documented in the JAMA Network Open 2025 study (n=29,919) represents a structural healthcare access inequity. Independent laboratories serve populations that hospital-based laboratories often do not reach. AI-driven claim recovery that reduces this denial disparity addresses a national healthcare equity concern.

**Healthcare Infrastructure Preservation**

The independent laboratory sector provides 32% of US diagnostic test volume. Systemic financial pressure from PAMA cuts and preventable denial rates threatens to accelerate the consolidation of this sector into large national reference laboratory networks, reducing the geographic distribution of diagnostic services and increasing turnaround times for community-serving tests.

**AI Governance Leadership**

Ardia Health Labs built its platform in native compliance with Texas SB 1188 (effective September 1, 2025) and Texas TRAIGA (HB 149, effective January 1, 2026) — the first healthcare-specific AI accountability law and the third state comprehensive AI governance law in the United States, respectively. The development of responsible AI governance practices in the healthcare administrative domain has national significance for the US AI regulatory framework.

- How payer clinical operations teams configure automated claim denial systems
- The documentation requirements and coverage criteria that trigger or prevent automated denials
- Prior authorization AI decision engine configuration and clinical criteria weighting

1. **Not available from public sources**: Payer claim edit libraries, coverage determination matrices, and prior authorization AI scoring criteria are proprietary and not disclosed in public payer documentation

Manasa Jampani's specific roles at United HealthGroup and Interwell Health placed her in proximity to payer clinical operations functions. ## Prong 3: Beneficial to the United States to Waive Labor Certification

### No Comparable Domestic Expertise Available

The labor certification process exists to protect US workers from displacement by foreign workers filling roles that qualified US workers are available to fill. Manasa Jampani's role cannot be filled by a US worker without her specific combination of:

- Direct payer-side clinical operations experience at United HealthGroup and Interwell Health (the specific organizations whose operational knowledge informs the DGP architecture's countermeasure design)
- Co-founder status and ownership stake in Ardia Health Labs (not a role that can be hired through PERM recruitment)

The labor certification process cannot identify a US worker who has simultaneously: (a) Manasa's specific payer clinical operations experience at the specific organizations that shaped her insight, AND (b) the entrepreneurial relationship to the company that makes her the appropriate person to advance this endeavor.

### Urgency of the National Need

PAMA 2027 creates a time-sensitive national need. If the reimbursement cuts take effect as projected, the consolidation pressure on independent laboratories will accelerate in 2027–2029. AI-driven claim recovery technology that addresses the denial burden before these cuts take effect has maximum national impact during this window. Delay in Manasa Jampani's continued contribution to this work — through a multi-year PERM process — would directly reduce the impact of this work on the national need.

### No Comparable Platform Exists

No existing commercially available AI platform addresses the specific combination of:
- MolDX/LCD/NCD/DEX Z-code compliance for molecular diagnostic billing
- Payer AI countermeasure design informed by payer-side operational intelligence
- ACO operations optimization for independent laboratory value-based care participation

Ardia Health Labs represents the only platform pursuing this specific combination. Manasa Jampani's continued involvement is essential to its success, and her success serves the national interest.

### Preserving Healthcare Infrastructure

Every independent laboratory that survives the PAMA 2027 cuts because it successfully recovered denied claims represents a diagnostic facility that continues serving its community. Manasa Jampani's work at Ardia Health Labs has direct implications for laboratory accessibility in rural, addiction medicine, and specialty healthcare communities — populations that depend on independent laboratories precisely because they lack hospital-system access.
