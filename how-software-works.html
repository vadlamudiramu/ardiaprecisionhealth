The 6-Step Autonomous Revenue Recovery Engine

Every denied lab claim moves through six AI-orchestrated stages. Each step is auditable, human-supervised per TX SB 1188, and executes in minutes — not weeks.

What makes Ardia different from manual billing

Traditional RCM cycles take 45–90 days per claim with human billers charging $118+ per appeal. Ardia's Neuro-Symbolic Sandwich Architecture processes the same claim in under 2 seconds, cross-references 847 active CMS LCDs, generates a clinically grounded appeal brief, and delivers it to the MAC — all for $2.50 per claim. The 47× cost advantage compounds across a lab processing 50,000+ claims per year.

1
Data Ingestion Layer

Claim & EOB Ingestion via EDI 835/837

When a payer denies a lab claim, they transmit a standardized ANSI X12 835 Electronic Remittance Advice (ERA) file. Ardia's ingestion pipeline continuously monitors your practice management system or clearinghouse for incoming 835 files, parses every denial transaction set, and loads structured claim data into the Ardia claims ledger within seconds of receipt.

EDI 835 / 837 Parsing

Ingests payer remittances, extracts CAS segments, claim adjustment reason codes (CO-, PR-, OA-), and PLB codes at the loop and segment level.

EHR / PMS Connectors

Native HL7 FHIR R4 connectors for Epic, Athena, eClinicalWorks, Kareo, and direct API integration with Office Ally, Change Healthcare, Availity clearinghouses.

HIPAA-Secure Ingestion

All PHI is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). U.S.-only data residency required by TX SB 1188 §4(c) — zero offshore processing.

Triage & Prioritization

Incoming denials are scored by dollar value, payer, denial code, and LAB-type (tox, molecular, PGx) and ranked in the priority queue for AI processing.

Neuro-Symbolic AI Core

AI Denial Classification & Viability Scoring

Ardia's Neuro-Symbolic Sandwich Architecture deploys three AI model tiers simultaneously. The top tier uses a large language model for natural language understanding of clinical context. The middle tier applies symbolic reasoning — rules-based logic over CMS coverage policies. The bottom tier uses a deep neural network trained on 2.4M historical claim outcomes to predict appeal success probability.

LLM Clinical Context Layer

Extracts clinical intent from free-text diagnostic notes, ICD-10 codes, and CPT code sequences. Understands why a test was ordered relative to the patient's condition.

Symbolic Rules Engine

847 active CMS Local Coverage Determinations (LCDs) encoded as formal logic rules. Maps each claim to applicable LCDs by payer, MAC jurisdiction, and CPT code.

ML Outcome Predictor

Trained on 2.4M historical claims. Predicts appeal success probability (0–100%) using 140+ features: payer behavior, MAC, denial code, CPT, diagnoses, and claim history.

Viability Threshold

Claims scoring ≥72% appeal confidence are queued for automated appeal. Claims below threshold are flagged for human review — never silently abandoned.

3
Retrieval-Augmented Generation

Evidence Retrieval from 14 Clinical Databases

Before drafting a single word of the appeal letter, the Ardia RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) system queries a curated corpus of 14 clinical and regulatory databases in parallel. It retrieves the exact LCD paragraph supporting the test, the relevant NCCN guideline citation, peer-reviewed PubMed evidence, and payer-specific policy language. All retrieved evidence is citation-linked and stored in the claim audit trail.

CMS Coverage Corpus

Full text of 847 active LCDs, all NCDs, and MAC-specific billing articles. Updated weekly via CMS Coverage Database API.

Clinical Guidelines

NCCN, ASCO, ACOG, ACS, CAP, AACC, and ASCP guidelines. Indexed at the paragraph level for precise citation retrieval.

PubMed Evidence

Real-time PubMed/MEDLINE query for peer-reviewed studies supporting medical necessity of the specific test and indication combination.

Appeals Case Law

Historical ALJ and DAB appeals decisions indexed by CPT code and denial reason. Winning precedent is auto-cited in the appeal brief.

4
Autonomous Document Generation

AI Appeal Brief Generation & Human Review

The AI drafts a structured appeal letter following CMS Level 1 Redetermination requirements: clinical summary, medical necessity justification with cited evidence, point-by-point rebuttal of the denial rationale, applicable LCD/NCD language, and supporting documentation checklist. Under Texas SB 1188, a licensed clinician or billing professional reviews and approves every appeal before submission — the human-in-the-loop gate is mandatory and audited.

CMS-Compliant Format

Drafts follow MAC-specific Level 1 redetermination templates. Auto-populated with claim number, patient info, provider NPI, and service dates.

Evidence Weaving

Retrieved clinical evidence is structured into a coherent narrative argument — not just appended. The AI reasons from evidence to conclusion like a trained appeals specialist.

TX SB 1188 Review Gate

Every AI-generated appeal is flagged for licensed professional review. The provider sees the draft, reviews cited evidence, and approves or edits before any submission.

Full Audit Trail

Every AI action, evidence retrieval, draft version, and human review event is timestamped and immutably logged for CMS audit defense and HIPAA compliance.

5
Automated Submission & Tracking

Multi-Channel Appeal Submission & Deadline Monitoring

Approved appeals are transmitted through the appropriate submission channel for each payer — EDI 277CA for electronic MACs, secure fax for commercial payers, and certified mail with tracking for paper-required submissions. The Ardia deadline engine monitors 120-day CMS appeal windows and commercial payer timely filing limits, auto-escalating any claim approaching its deadline with priority alerts.

Electronic Submission

Direct EDI transmission to Palmetto GBA, CGS, Novitas, WPS, and NGS MACs. Acknowledgment tracking via 999/277CA transaction sets.

Deadline Engine

Tracks 120-day CMS redetermination windows and all commercial payer timely filing limits. Auto-alerts 30, 14, and 7 days before each deadline.

Level Escalation

If Level 1 is denied, the system auto-prepares Level 2 (QIC), Level 3 (ALJ), and Level 4 (DAB) packets. Each escalation is triggered within 24 hours of adverse decision.

Real-Time Status

Dashboard tracks every appeal's position in the appeals process. Providers receive push notifications on status changes, decisions, and payment notifications.

89% Level 1 win rate · 94% within 60 days
6
Payment Reconciliation & Intelligence

ERA Payment Auto-Posting & Revenue Intelligence

When the payer approves the appeal, they transmit a new 835 ERA with the payment. Ardia's reconciliation engine matches the approved ERA to the original claim, auto-posts the payment to your PMS, calculates the recovery net of Ardia's success fee, and closes the claim loop. The entire recovery — from original denial to payment post — is logged as a case study in your lab's AI learning corpus, improving future prediction accuracy.

Auto-Reconciliation

Inbound 835 approval ERAs are matched to open appeal cases within seconds. Payment is auto-posted to the originating claim in your PMS without manual intervention.

Revenue Analytics

Every recovery feeds the Ardia analytics engine: denial trends by payer, CPT performance, appeal win rates by MAC, and projected future recovery pipeline.

Continuous Learning

Each resolved case — won or lost — refines the ML outcome predictor. Your lab's model improves with every claim. Network-wide learning across all Ardia clients raises the floor for everyone.

Success-Fee Billing

Ardia charges 15% of recovered revenue only. No recovery = no fee. Monthly statements itemize every recovery, Ardia fee, and net-to-lab amount with full claim-level detail.

Avg $3,200 recovered per tox claim · $47× ROI
